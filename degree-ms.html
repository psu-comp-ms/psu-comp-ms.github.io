Degree Requirements / MS Program

COMP-MS Degree Requirements

A total of 30 graduate credits (400-level or above) is required for the degree Master of Science in Computer Science. A minimum grade point average of 3.0 must be earned for course work approved by the program taken as a graduate student.


Required Courses (12 credits)

  • COMP 505 Theory of Computation
  • COMP 511 Design and Analysis of Algorithms
  • COMP 512 Advanced Operating Systems
  • COMP 519 Advanced Topics in Database Management Systems

Additionally, students are required to complete either a thesis or a paper according to one of the two options described below. Students who believe that they have completed a course substantially similar to one of the specific course requirements may apply to have their previous work evaluated for the purpose of exemption from that requirement. If the exemption is granted, another approved course shall be taken in place of that required course. The remaining 18 credits must be completed according to one of the following options.


1. Thesis Option (18 credits)

Research into a specific computer science problem, development of a scholarly thesis, and an oral defense. To complete a thesis, you must form a committee of at least 3 Graduate Faculty members, one of whom must serve as the main supervisor of your research. This option requires:

  • 6 credits of COMP 600
  • 3 additional credits from approved 500-level electives in computer science courses
  • 9 credits from approved 400- and 500-level electives in computer science, mathematics, engineering, and information systems courses. A list of 400-level courses that can be used for graduate credit can be found in the Courses section.

2. Paper Option (18 credits)

In-depth study of a specific computer science problem, development of a written scholarly paper or project, and an oral presentation. The scholarly paper must be supervised by a Graduate Faculty member and a second reader. This option requires:

  • 3 credits of COMP 594
  • 9 credits from approved 500-level electives in computer science, mathematics, engineering, and information systems courses
  • 6 credits from approved 400- and 500-level electives in computer science, mathematics, engineering, and information systems courses. A list of 400-level courses that can be used for graduate credit can be found in the Courses section.

Program Policies

The following policies are strictly enforced.

  • During the course of your study, you must also complete a two-part responsible conduct of research requirement. See the RECR Requirements section below for more information.
  • Students must be aware of the importance of academic integrity↗. Any violation, intentional or unintentional, will not be tolerated and will be subject to proper sanctions↗ according to G-9: Academic Integrity↗.
  • A maximum of 9 transfer credits will be allowed for course work completed as a graduate student at another institution. See the Transfer Credit section below for more information.
  • Students are required to complete their program of study within 8 years of the beginning of the first semester of enrollment in the COMP program (GCAC-632↗).
  • 500-level courses outside COMP will not count toward your COMP-MS degree unless you have a pre-approval↗ from the COMP program.

The Responsible and Ethical Conduct of Research (RECR) program↗ is designed to create an awareness of ethical principles and established professional norms in the performance of all activities related to scholarship and research. Ultimately, our goal is to further foster trust among scholars and researchers and to increase the public’s support for research.

Penn State requires RECR for all graduate students, postdocs, and new faculty to ensure the ethical conduct of research and scholarship at the University. In addition, undergraduate students participating in research must also participate in RECR training.

There are two main components to fulfill RECR requirements at Penn State:

Part 1. CITI Training (online)

Completion of the Responsible Conduct of Research (RCR) module within the CITI program is required for all graduate students. Access CITI program courses via citi.psu.edu↗. Using your Penn State Access Account credentials, log in to CITI for University Park, Commonwealth, and other non-Hershey personnel. Once you complete the program, download the Complete Certificate and submit it to the Professor-in-Charge.

Part 2. Five Educational Programming Hours

An additional 5 RECR hours are required beyond the CITI training. The RECR hours should be earned within the first 2 years of graduate school. The 5 RECR educational programming hours for COMP program is built into COMP 594. If you are following the Paper option, you automatically complete 5 RECR educational programming hours when you take COMP 594. If you are following the Thesis option, you must attend the first 2 weeks of COMP 594 to complete 5 RECR educational programming hours. You must complete CITI online training before attempting the 5 RECR hours.

A maximum of 9 credits will be allowed for course work completed as a graduate student at another institution. Transfer credits must meet the following criteria:

  • Must have been earned at a regionally accredited U.S. institution or an officially recognized degree-granting international institution
  • Must have been earned within 5 years preceding entry into the program
  • Must not have applied toward any previous degree requirements
  • Must appear on an official transcript
  • Must be equivalent to a 400- or 500-level course at Penn State
  • Must be of A or B grade value
  • B- grades and Pass/Fail grades are not transferable

For a detailed description of the transfer credit policy, see GCAC-309: Transfer Credit↗.
