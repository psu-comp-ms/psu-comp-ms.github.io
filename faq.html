FAQ

How do I apply? Penn State University requires all applications to be done electronically. You can access online applications through Graduate School Admissions ↗.

What are the fees for applying? Please go to the Graduate School admissions FAQ ↗ for information about application fees and related admissions questions.
What are the tuition fees and other expenses? You can check the Penn State Tuition page ↗ for up-to-date information on tuition and various other charges, including housing costs, activity fees, student surcharge fees, and the Information Technology Fee.
What are the entrance requirements? The entrance requirements are explained in Entrance Requirements. Please note that Penn State University does not recognize the three-year BS degree in Computer Science.
What if I do not meet the entrance requirements? If you are missing only a few of the courses necessary to meet the entrance requirements, typically two or three, you can still apply for admission; however, you will probably be granted provisional admission. If you are missing more than four entrance requirements, you are advised to take courses as a non-degree graduate student until you have made up some of the deficiencies. Some courses taken as a non-degree graduate student can later be transferred into the program.
What if my undergraduate degree is not in Computer Science or a closely related field? No, you do not need an undergraduate degree in Computer Science to apply. However, if your previous degree is not in Computer Science or a closely related field, there will be courses you need to take before applying to the program.
What if I do not have an undergraduate degree in any field? If you are close to finishing your BS degree, you can be admitted provisionally into the MS program pending completion of your BS degree. This is the only exception allowed to the requirement that a student have a BS degree from an accredited institution.
What materials are necessary for admission?

Students are not required to take the GRE to apply for admission.

Admission to the program is based on review of the following items:

  1. Transcripts of all undergraduate and graduate education. A minimum GPA of 2.75 on a 4.0 scale is required for admission.
  2. Two letters of reference, at least one of which is from an academic source.
  3. A letter outlining significant work experience and academic and career objectives.
  4. International applicants must submit TOEFL or IELTS scores unless they qualify for an approved exemption based on prior degrees earned in designated English-speaking countries. The minimum acceptable TOEFL score is 550 paper-based, 213 computer-based, or 80 internet-based with a 19 on the speaking section. The minimum IELTS composite score is 6.5.
  5. A copy of an official transcript from each college or university attended, except Penn State University, must be submitted as part of the online application. If you are offered admission and accept, official transcripts must later be sent from each institution.

What are the application deadlines?

We have no set deadlines for graduate applications. Once we receive your completed paperwork from the admissions office, we can typically review applications and send letters in approximately two to three weeks.

Although the department continues to review applications, the University recommends the following deadlines for international students to allow time for visa processing:

  • Fall semester: May 31
  • Spring semester: September 30
  • Summer semester: February 28
What is the difference between provisional and full admission? If students have not met all entrance requirements, they may receive provisional admission. This means they still have deficiencies to remove. Any deficiencies must be removed during the first two semesters if you are full-time, or within the first 15 credits if you are part-time. Full admission depends on your ability to make up the deficiencies while maintaining a 2.75 average for those courses. The transition from provisional to full admission is typically done automatically once the requirements have been met.
How do I determine what will transfer into the program?

You must confer with your advisor about transferring any courses into the program. For Penn State policy, see GCAC-309 Transfer Credit ↗. In general, the following rules apply:

  • Credits transferred to Penn State must have been earned within five years preceding entry into the degree program.
  • At most, 15 non-degree graduate credits from a Penn State location and at most 10 non-degree graduate credits from a non-Penn State location can be transferred into a graduate degree program, subject to program approval.
  • A student can transfer up to 9 Penn State undergraduate credits into a graduate program if those credits were not used in fulfilling undergraduate degree requirements and are at the 400 or 500 level. No undergraduate credits from other schools can be transferred.

All transfer credits must be approved by the student’s advisor.
How do I get financial aid? There is a limited number of scholarships and research grants available. Many students work full-time and take classes part-time, and some employers offer tuition reimbursement. For more options, contact the Financial Aid Office at 717-948-6307 or visit the PSH Financial Aid Office ↗. For graduate-specific aid information, see Financial Aid for Graduate Students ↗.
What do I do if I want a Graduate School assistantship? Full-time graduate students who are interested in an assistantship should contact the graduate program coordinator. Students must be nominated for an assistantship by their program coordinator.
What are my housing options?

The only on-campus housing currently available is in the student apartments available through the Residence Life office ↗. See the campus map ↗ for a map of campus and pictures of these apartments.

Many students also live off campus in Middletown, Harrisburg, and surrounding areas. One popular apartment complex is The Village of Pineford ↗ in Middletown. You may also want to review Middletown Borough ↗ for local housing information.
What do I do when I first get to campus? The department has prepared a checklist for new graduate students. Please see To Do List for New Graduate Computer Science Students.
Who is my advisor?

There are basically two advisors for graduate students. Your academic advisor is the faculty member assigned to help you get started in the Computer Science graduate program, especially with course scheduling, transferring potential courses, and making sure that you satisfy degree requirements.

You should have received a letter with your academic advisor’s name and contact information when you were accepted into the program. You should meet with your academic advisor before every semester to register for your courses. Do not simply stop by the office and ask for a signature; instead, make an appointment and have a discussion so you and your advisor are both clear on your goals.

After you have started coursework, you will need to choose a project advisor. This advisor will help you with your master’s project.
How do I decide which classes to take? Your academic advisor should always be consulted regarding your class scheduling. Once you have selected your project advisor, that advisor will take over academic advising duties in addition to helping you with your project and defense.
How do I choose a project advisor?

Once you decide to begin working on your master’s project, it is a good idea to talk to all of the Computer Science faculty members. Some students know exactly what they want to do for their project, but many do not, so it is very important to get to know the faculty.

  • Take courses from professors in the area in which you are interested.
  • Make appointments to speak with professors and review their research interests on the faculty pages ↗.
  • Speak with other graduate students.

Your project advisor must be a member of the Computer Science Department at Penn State Harrisburg. Start talking to potential advisors early, because it may take longer to find a project than you expect.

Once selected, you and your advisor should set goals, communicate expectations, and establish a timeline for completion. Discuss coursework, publication of papers, presentations, your defense, meeting times, your graduate committee, and the necessary paperwork for your project and graduation.
How can I see what classes are offered? You can view all courses offered in the Penn State system on LionPATH by clicking the students, faculty, and staff login button and signing in with your Penn State credentials. Then select Find Classes and enter the appropriate semester, campus location, and department. This is a good way to view the schedule because it shows how many seats are left in each class.
How can I register for classes? You can register in person at Enrollment Services in the Swatara Building, or you can use the LionPATH system. For any registration method, you will need the schedule number for each class you wish to take. You can obtain the schedule numbers from the schedule of classes or from the web.
What should I do if a class I want to take is full? Contact the staff assistant at (717) 948-6081.
Where can I get information regarding transcripts, etc.? You can get your transcript in LionPATH.
What are the standards of academic performance? As a graduate student in the Computer Science MS program, you must maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0 for coursework approved by the program taken as a graduate student. When a student’s GPA for any semester falls below 3.00, the student should consult with the advisor immediately. A student whose GPA drops below 3.00 for two consecutive semesters is normally dropped from the program. At the 400 and 500 levels, grades of A, B, and C denote graduate credit, whereas D and F are failing grades for graduate students.
What is considered full-time for a graduate student? To be considered full-time, graduate students must take a minimum of 9 credits per semester during the fall and spring semesters. The minimum required number of credits for full-time status in the summer is 5 credit hours, or 9 credits for financial aid. See the University Registrar ↗ for more information about enrollment status at Penn State.
Can I take courses over? If so, what happens to my GPA? Yes, you can take courses over. However, the original grade is not erased from your transcript, and the original grade will also contribute to your cumulative GPA.
Can I get help with writing papers, etc.? The Russell E. Horn Learning Center ↗ in SEC 201 provides help to students with writing and other course-related issues. Services are provided free to students enrolled in Penn State Harrisburg courses.
What is the master’s defense? At some point while working with your advisor on your master’s project, you will be ready to schedule your defense, or oral examination. Every graduate student is expected to give a presentation of the project and answer questions regarding the work. Your committee members will be present for the defense, and other faculty members or students may also attend.
What do I need to do to prepare for the defense? You and your project advisor need to work out a timeline for your defense. There is no specific paperwork to fill out for the defense, but there are deadlines, so work closely with your project advisor. Other members of your committee need time to review your paper before the defense, and you should allow at least two weeks for that review.
What do I need to do to prepare for graduation?

The most important step is to file an intent to graduate so that you are added to the graduation list. Early in the semester in which you intend to graduate, you must register your intent using the Graduating this Semester application in the LionPATH system ↗.

Confirm the activation period on the academic calendar ↗ to make sure you submit the intent during the correct dates.

There is rarely substantial revision to your written paper after your defense, but typically there will be some modifications required to produce the final draft.
What are the Sun Lab machines used for? We encourage you to use the Sun Lab machines in W210A and W210B Olmsted for all of your programming assignments, although you may also program under Windows for most assignments. The machines run on a distribution of Linux, Ubuntu ↗, and are useful for general-purpose computing such as web development, email, word processing and productivity work using OpenOffice, and many other tasks.
How do I get a Sun Lab account? You should automatically be given an account in the lab. If you are unable to access the machines, please see the staff assistant for Math and Computer Science in W255 Olmsted.
How can I get into the Sun Lab? To enter the Sun Lab in W210A and W210 Olmsted, you will need to be added to the access list. After that, you can use your Penn State ID card to unlock the door. If you are unable to access the lab, please see the staff assistant for Math and Computer Science in W255 Olmsted.
How can I learn how to use the Sun Lab machines and Unix? One helpful resource is the SUN Lab Usage Guide. The book UNIX in a Nutshell, Arnold Robbins, O’Reilly, 1999, ISBN 1-56592-427-4, is also a useful reference for anyone who is new to Unix. Finally, more experienced Unix users working in the lab are often willing to answer short questions.
What uses of the Sun Lab machines are appropriate and ethical? All of the usual Penn State rules apply, including the student conduct policies ↗, as well as additional rules from the Computer Science Program detailed in the Policy on Ethical Uses of Computing Resources. Please also review the Penn State Acceptable Use of University Information Resources ↗. The short answer is that viewing or altering another user’s data without permission is never allowed, and any commercial use of Penn State computing resources is prohibited.
Can I use the Sun Lab machines from home or some other location? You can access the Sun Lab machines remotely using SSH and SFTP. This VPN article ↗ provides information on connecting to Penn State using a VPN. The department does not condone using Telnet or FTP because those protocols send your password across the network unencrypted. The usage guide contains a list of hostnames for the Sun Lab machines. Secure Shell is available for free for non-commercial use through ITS software resources ↗.
